How It Works

What a 6-week engagement looks like

Not a program. Not a protocol. A structured redesign of the environment producing your current results.

01

Environmental Audit

Session 1 is diagnostic. We map the exact constraints producing your current plateau — task, environment, and organism constraints together. You leave knowing precisely what's creating the gap, not guessing at it.

02

Constraint Redesign

Sessions 2–4 are applied. We redesign the environment — not what you do, but the conditions under which you do it. One constraint changed per session. Small enough to execute. Large enough to shift the pattern.

03

Transfer & Integration

Sessions 5–6 are integration. The shift has to stick across contexts — not just in the environment we designed together, but in the real conditions you perform in. We test it, adjust it, and anchor it.

04

Between Sessions

Each session ends with one constraint to implement — not a homework list, a single designed change. You apply it. The environment produces the feedback. We review it next session. The results are the data.

Who This Is For

This is not for everyone.

Specifically, this is for the person who already knows enough — and is ready to change the environment instead of consuming more knowledge.

If you need someone to tell you what to do, this isn't the right fit. If you're ready to understand why your current environment is producing current results — and design better constraints — it is.

  • Coaches who've been coaching for years and still can't explain exactly why some athletes transfer and others don't.
  • Athletes who perform perfectly in practice and fall apart in competition — and have tried everything they've been told to try.
  • Creators and entrepreneurs who are smart, driven, doing the work — and still hitting the same ceiling every quarter.
  • Anyone who can feel the gap between their current results and what they know they're capable of — and is ready to diagnose it properly.

  • People who want a prescriptive protocol and someone to tell them exactly what to do each week.
